Rabi'a al-Adawiyya - Muslim Sufi Saint

About Rabi'a al-Adawiyya

Rabi'a al-Adawiyya (717-801 CE) was a prominent Muslim Sufi saint and mystic who lived during the Golden Age of Islam. She was born in Basra, Iraq, and her deep spirituality and profound poetry continue to inspire people around the world. Rabi'a's life was characterized by unwavering devotion to God and a radical love that transcended traditional religious boundaries.

Teachings of Rabi'a al-Adawiyya

Rabi'a al-Adawiyya's teachings revolved around the following principles:

  • Love for God: She emphasized the importance of selfless love and devotion to God, teaching that pure love for the Divine should be the ultimate goal of every individual.
  • Sincerity and Detachment: Rabi'a believed in the absolute sincerity of one's devotion to God, unburdened by any desire for reward or fear of punishment. She practiced detachment from worldly possessions and desires.
  • The Concept of Annihilation (Fana): She advocated for the complete annihilation of the self in God's love, where the lover and the Beloved become one.
  • Compassion and Service: Rabi'a's love extended to all beings, and she was known for her acts of kindness and compassion towards others.
